TCP/IP

TCP/IP stands for Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol. It is a set of communication protocols used to interconnect network devices on the internet. TCP/IP specifies how data should be packetized, addressed, transmitted, routed, and received.

TCP/IP was developed in the 1970s by Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn. It has since become the standard protocol for communication over the internet, enabling different types of devices and networks to communicate with each other.

Key components of TCP/IP include:

  • Transmission Control Protocol (TCP): Responsible for ensuring reliable data transmission between devices. It breaks data into packets, sends them to the destination, and ensures they are reassembled correctly.
  • Internet Protocol (IP): Responsible for addressing and routing packets of data so that they can travel across networks and arrive at the correct destination.
